Example 1:
Problem: Janet's ducks lay 16 eggs per day. She eats three for breakfast and bakes four into muffins. She sells the rest for $2 each. How much does she make per day?

\reasoningbox{{
Step 1: Janet starts with 16 eggs per day
Step 2: She eats 3 eggs, so 16 - 3 = 13 eggs remaining
Step 3: She bakes 4 eggs into muffins, so 13 - 4 = 9 eggs remaining
Step 4: She sells the remaining 9 eggs for $2 each
Step 5: Total earnings = 9 × $2 = $18
}}

\answerbox{{
18
}}

Example 2:
Problem: A store has 20 apples. They sell 8 in the morning and 5 in the afternoon. How many are left?

\reasoningbox{{
Step 1: Store starts with 20 apples
Step 2: Sells 8 in morning: 20 - 8 = 12 apples left
Step 3: Sells 5 in afternoon: 12 - 5 = 7 apples left
}}

\answerbox{{
7
}}

Example 3:
Problem: If John reads 3 books per week, how many books does he read in 4 weeks?

\reasoningbox{{
Step 1: John reads 3 books per week
Step 2: In 4 weeks, he reads: 3 × 4 = 12 books
}}

\answerbox{{
12
}}
